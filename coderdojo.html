CoderDojo NYC Website Redesign

CoderDojo NYC is non-profit organization hosting free STEAM workshops for youth aged 7-17. I am thrilled to offer my help as a volunteer designer to help them refresh their existing website.

Challenges
  • CoderDojo receives an overwhelming amount of emails from both parents and future volunteers asking questions about how to sign up. Because of the organization is 100% volunteer based company (no full-time staff), they don’t have enough resources to answer such emails.

Because of the organization is 100% volunteer based company (no full-time staff), they don’t have enough resources to answer such emails. Due to the organization’s unique situations, CoderDojo NYC has a rather unique signing up process for events. This process, however, did not get explained clearly on the website.
  • CoderDojo NYC has two newsletters and it was not clearly marked or explained on the website. Parents and volunteers often sign up for the wrong mailing list.
Goals
  • Refresh the website's visual style.
  • Better explain the sign up processes for both parents and volunteers.
  • Reorganize site content to improve findability.
  • Reduce the number of inqury emails.
Results
The stakeholders are satisfied with my service and the final design. This website is currently being developed.

Process

Scenario

To help me think from a site user’s perspective, I created two user scenarios. These are the two primary use case of the two user group that my design will be optimized for.

Information Architecture

I started by creating a content inventory of all information Coderdojo presented at their current website. Then I set up an online card sorting project using Optimal Workshop. I have recruited 11 participants (2 of them abandoned the sorting during their sessions, but I've included some of their data in my result analysis). I analyzed the results using the Similarity Matrix and Dendrograms provided by Optimal Workshop.

Based on the analysis, I designed the new global navigation.

And also reorganized the site content into a new sitemap.

Ideation

After button down the Information Architecture, I started brainstorming ideas, sketching and wireframing. I started with quick and messy sketches to generate as many ideas as possible. After I got something I really like, I started to sketch with a bit more details.

Mid-fi Mockups

I fleshed out a few key pages and created mid-fi mockups to gather input from the stakeholders.

Then I consolidated the feedbacks from my client and finalized the visual design after a few more iterations.
